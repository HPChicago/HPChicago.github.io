“It is good to have an end to journey toward; but it is the journey that matters, in the end.” - Ursula K. Le Guin

It seemed that time was passing by very fast. I couldn't have believed that it had been 10 hours since plane took off from Europe. Taking into the account the fact that I have never flown on planes before, every second was unbelievably exciting! My seat was right next to the window. I chose it on purpose. Like any other man who flies first time, I wanted to see through that window as much as I could, not thinking about how much time it would take me to get to the closest hallway (which I should have considered as well). At some point, I was so dehydrated that my lips were dry, I simply couldn’t get off my seat to find water, because there were 5 sleeping people on my way, and staff members disappeared as soon as lunch was over.
